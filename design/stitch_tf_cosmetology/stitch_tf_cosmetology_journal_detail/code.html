Case Study 09-Alpha: The Bio-Availability of Liposomal Delivery

Document ID

LAB-CS-09A

Lead Scientist Dr. Sang Hyun Moh
Publication Date October 24, 2024
Reading Time 12 min read
Clinical Classification Bio-Molecular Science

Fig 1.1: Preparation of lipid-based emulsion under controlled thermal conditions.

01. Executive Summary

The efficacy of active cosmetic ingredients is inherently limited by their ability to penetrate the epidermal barrier. Liposomal delivery systems represent a paradigm shift in bio-availability, utilizing phospholipid bilayers to encapsulate potent actives. This report examines the specific absorption rates of Protocol 09-Alpha, a proprietary liposomal matrix designed for sustained release.

format_quote
"The architecture of a lipid bilayer is as much a work of art as it is of science."

02. Methodology

The study utilized a randomized, triple-blind control group. Synthetic skin membranes (Strat-M®) were treated with both traditional aqueous solutions and the liposomal formulation 09-Alpha. Absorption was measured via high-performance liquid chromatography (HPLC) at intervals of 2, 6, 12, and 24 hours.

03. Results and Observations

Preliminary data indicates a 440% increase in transdermal retention when compared to traditional delivery mechanisms. The liposomal structure remained intact through the stratum corneum, releasing its cargo only upon reaching the deeper viable epidermis.
